Understanding Your Recent Electricity Bill

Hello Larry, we've reviewed your latest billing statement. While your energy supply price is successfully locked in, we noticed your Delivery Charges (TDSP) spiked this month due to a peak in demand. This document explains why this happened and how we can lower these costs together.

Note: Your "Fixed Rate" covers the cost of electricity. Delivery fees from CenterPoint are regulated pass-through costs that vary based on your facility's peak demand (kVA).

The Cost Driver: Peak Demand

This Month's Peak 108 kVA

Target Peak: Under 60 kVA

"Think of kVA as the size of the water pipe CenterPoint had to build for your facility. Because you turned on all your machines at once, they charged you for a massive pipe all month long."

Potential Savings: ~$450.00 / mo

By reducing your peak demand spike.

Action Plan: Lowering Your Delivery Bill

Step 1: Operational Changes
  • 01

    Stagger Machine Start-ups: Don't turn on all plating tanks and HVAC units at 8:00 AM. Stagger by 15-minute intervals.

  • 02

    Manage Lunch Restarts: If the floor shuts down for lunch, stagger the ramp-up at 1:00 PM to avoid a secondary peak.

Step 2: Technical Upgrades
  • 03

    Power Factor Correction: Installing capacitor banks can clean up "dirty" power and drop your kVA charges by 10% or more.

  • 04

    Demand Controllers: Automated systems can "shed" load (like AC units) momentarily to keep you under your kVA target.
