# Nodal Point Company Context (For GPT Chats)

## One-Line Summary
Nodal Point helps Texas businesses lower electricity costs by analyzing energy bills, exposing hidden charges, and guiding better energy agreements.

## What We Do
- We review commercial power bills and find where money is leaking.
- We break down complex utility and supplier pricing into plain language.
- We help clients choose or renegotiate energy agreements based on real cost drivers, not just advertised rate-per-kWh.
- We monitor ERCOT market signals (like volatility and demand-related charges) to reduce future risk.

## The Core Problem We Solve
Most companies focus only on the energy rate, but a big part of the bill comes from demand charges, pass-through fees, and tariff structure. Those hidden pieces can quietly drive costs up. We surface those hidden costs and build a smarter agreement strategy.

## Who We Serve
- Texas commercial and industrial electricity buyers
- Multi-site operators that need one clear view of energy performance
- Teams that want both software insights and expert guidance

## How We Work (Simple Version)
1. Client uploads a bill or usage data.
2. We run a forensic analysis to isolate true costs and hidden risk.
3. We recommend actions: optimize usage profile, fix agreement structure, or renegotiate terms.
4. We support execution and ongoing market-aware strategy.

## Why We’re Different
- Forensics, not generic broker sales talk
- Signal over noise: we filter out distractions and focus on high-impact cost drivers
- We combine software + human expertise
- Built specifically for ERCOT/Texas market complexity

## Services / Product Language
- Bill Forensic Analysis ("Debug My Bill")
- Tariff and pass-through fee analysis
- Demand charge and 4CP exposure analysis
- Energy agreement strategy and negotiation support
- Market outlook and risk-aware planning

## Tone & Positioning for GPT Responses
When describing Nodal Point, use this style:
- Clear and direct
- Practical, not hypey
- Focused on cost reduction, risk reduction, and better agreements
- Explain complex energy topics in plain English

Avoid this style:

- "We are a generic CRM"
- Overly technical explanations without business impact
