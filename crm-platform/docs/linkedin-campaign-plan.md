# Nodal Point — LinkedIn Content Campaign Brief

---

## 1. Campaign Overview

**Campaign Name:** `SIGNAL_FEED` — The Forensic Energy Intelligence Series

**One-sentence summary:** Establish Lewis Patterson and Nodal Point as the definitive authority on hidden cost leakage in commercial energy contracts, converting energy brokers and commercial CFOs from passive scrollers to pipeline.

**Primary Objective:** Build a warm, high-intent audience of 500+ energy brokers and commercial energy decision-makers following both pages within 90 days — producing 3–5 qualified platform demo requests per week by Day 60.

**Secondary Objectives:**
- Position Nodal Point as the anti-CRM: a forensic intelligence platform, not a spreadsheet
- Establish Lewis as the most credible tariff forensics voice on LinkedIn in ERCOT/deregulated markets
- Generate content assets that double as sales collateral and Foundry AI training material

## 2. Target Audience

### Primary: Energy Brokers & Advisors
> Broker at an independent energy consultancy or retail supplier who is competing on rate comparisons alone, losing deals to firms that can explain the tariff — not just quote it. They discover content through LinkedIn, industry groups, and ERCOT/EIA news. They care about credibility, tools that make them look smarter to clients, and closing deals faster.

- **Pain:** They can't explain demand charges, 4CP, or ratchet floors in a CFO meeting without looking uncertain
- **Desire:** To be the broker who finds what others miss — the margin-extractor, not the rate-quoter
- **Buying stage:** Awareness → Consideration (they don't know a platform like this exists)

### Secondary: Commercial CFOs & Energy Managers
> CFO or energy manager at a manufacturing plant, cold storage facility, or commercial real estate portfolio in a deregulated market (Texas, PJM, etc.). They're being quoted energy rates they can't verify and suspect they're being overcharged but have no forensic tools.

- **Pain:** Every broker they talk to sounds the same. They can't tell if the contract structure is working against them.
- **Desire:** A single number — "here's the hidden exposure in your current contract"
- **Buying stage:** Awareness (they don't know tariff forensics is a thing)

### Tertiary: Other Founders & B2B SaaS Builders
> Follows Lewis for the builder narrative, not the energy content. Amplifies posts. Indirect brand awareness.

**Why:** Founders with a clear POV build more trust faster than brand pages. Your voice is the product's credibility. Energy brokers follow people, not logos.

**Format recommendations:**
- Long-form text posts (no links in post — LinkedIn suppresses reach)

## 8. Post Format Rules (Nodal Point Voice)

These apply to every post on both channels:

```
STRUCTURE (personal posts)
─────────────────────────
Line 1:   The hook — one sentence. Contrarian. Specific. No question marks.
Lines 2–4: Dead air (short lines or line breaks to force the "see more" click)
Body:      The forensic insight. Specific numbers. No vague claims.
           Use monospace-style formatting for data (dashes, colons, indents)
Close:     One-sentence implication. What does this mean for the broker?
CTA:       Rare. Only in Phases 2-3. Never "like and share."

EXAMPLE
───────
The ratchet floor in most commercial contracts charges you for peak demand
you never used.

Most brokers don't explain this.

Most CFOs don't know it exists.

Here's how it works:

  Contract ratchet floor:  70%
  Actual peak this month:  410 kW
  Billed demand:           490 kW  ← you never consumed this
  Monthly overcharge:      ~$1,800
  Annual exposure:         $21,600

The broker who can surface this number in a CFO meeting
wins the contract.

That's tariff forensics. Not rate comparison.
```
