Discovery Questions

Use this when you want the call to feel bigger than a rate quote. The job is to learn the current setup, whether they trust it, and what could bite them next.

print ready Generated April 30, 2026
What you need to uncover
Do not lead with "are you responsible for electricity?"
Lead with the reason for the call, then pull them into the bigger picture. You want the supplier, the expiration, the cents per kWh, the all-in bill, and anything coming that could create pain.

Listen for
The lines that matter
  • "I do not know when it expires."
  • "We have just been rolling it over."
  • "Nobody has looked at the bill in a while."
  • "We had a few ugly summer months."
  • "We are expanding / moving / adding equipment."

Discovery Ladder

Situation - Pain - Future risk
  1. 01
    Who are you using today? If they are not sure, ask who handles the renewal paperwork or the last bill review.
  2. 02
    When does the current agreement expire? If they do not know the exact day, ask for the month and whether it has been reviewed recently.
  3. 03
    Do you know what rate they are making you pay lately? I mean the cents per kWh piece, not just the all-in bill.
  4. 04
    Roughly what are you paying all-in right now? If the exact number is fuzzy, ask for the latest bill amount or a ballpark range.
  5. 05
    How confident are you in the current deal? That tells you whether they trust it or are just living with it.
  6. 06
    If nothing changed before renewal, what would worry you most? Say worry or concern.
Transition to the close
Keep it simple. Move from discovery to one next step, not a long pitch.
1. "Based on what you said, the next step is to check the bill and the expiration timing."
2. "If the setup looks fine, we say that. If there is an issue, we catch it before renewal forces a rushed decision."
3. "Would you rather send the bill over, or would it be easier to walk through it together for 10 minutes?"
Objection pivots
Already have a broker. "Totally fine. I’m not trying to replace anyone. I just want to see if the setup deserves a second look."
Send me info. "Happy to, but I’d rather know what matters before I send the wrong thing."
Too early. "Fair. Usually the earlier look is where you still have options."
Call tips
Lead with the reason. Say why you called before you ask the first question.
Separate rate from bill. Ask for the cents per kWh, then ask what the all-in bill looks like.
Do not stack angles. Use one clear lane at a time: renewal, budget, growth, or future pain.
