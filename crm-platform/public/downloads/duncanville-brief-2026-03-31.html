What the call told us

Marqeis said the district is still under contract and is not planning to move immediately. That is the right answer for procurement. It is not the right answer for bill visibility. The district should use the window now to understand whether there is a demand ratchet, a peak-demand rider, or summer load behavior that could carry cost forward even before the contract ends.

Key point. We are not asking Duncanville ISD to switch contracts today. We are asking for the latest bill and enough detail to see whether the current structure is already creating unnecessary pressure on the budget.

Districts usually start looking 6 to 8 months before expiration. That is the right time to compare options. It is also the right time to verify what the bill is doing right now.

Why this matters to a school district

Comfort is mandatory.Waste is optional.

Duncanville ISD does not need a flashy pitch. It needs a practical way to reduce operating noise across campuses, keep classrooms comfortable, and give leadership a clean story they can stand behind.

Most of the early wins are not in replacement work. They are in controls, scheduling, setpoints, fault detection, and right-sized equipment decisions that lower cost while keeping the buildings stable during Texas heat.

If the district is already planning 6 to 8 months ahead on procurement, now is the time to gather the bill data and identify any HVAC or controls work that should be bundled into the next review cycle.
Building Controls

Smart controls and building automation give Duncanville a way to manage multiple campuses with one operating view instead of chasing issues building by building.

  • Centralized Management: One dashboard can simplify district operations and keep leadership informed.
  • Automated Savings: Schedules, resets, and demand limiting can run every day without adding more manual work.
  • Alarms & FDD: Fault detection helps find comfort or cost problems before they turn into complaints.
  • Open Protocols: BACnet and Modbus keep the district flexible instead of locked into one narrow system.
HVAC

HVAC is where comfort and cost meet. The goal is not to spend less on the buildings. The goal is to stop paying for wasted run-time, oversized equipment, and poor sequencing.

  • Right-Sized Solutions: Load analysis helps match equipment to the actual building need.
  • Operational Efficiency: Better sequences and retro-commissioning cut out avoidable waste.
  • Indoor Air Quality: Filtration and ventilation upgrades can improve conditions without excess energy use.
  • Lifecycle Value: Lower operating expense and better control usually extend asset life.
